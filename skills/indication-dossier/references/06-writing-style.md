# Writing Style

Write in the style of an industry-trained analyst presenting the
facts clearly and completely.

## Inline citation format

Cite every factual claim with an inline markdown link:
`[descriptive claim text](source_url)`. Write every link as `[title](url)`;
when there is no natural claim text, use the source name and document type
as the title.

Use numbered references `[1]` only when the same source is cited 5+ times or
when citing several sources for one claim.

## What to cite

Always cite: quantitative data (prevalence, efficacy metrics, market size) ·
clinical endpoints and results · competitive intelligence (stage, timing) ·
dates (approvals, LOE, completion estimates) · safety data · patent numbers.

Don't cite: general medical knowledge, your own analytical interpretations,
or calculations you perform from cited inputs (cite the inputs).

## Link specificity

Link to the exact page, never a homepage.

- ClinicalTrials.gov: `https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT########`
- PubMed: `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/<PMID>/` (prefer PMID over DOI redirect)
- Company: the specific press release or presentation URL
- Patents: `https://patents.google.com/patent/US########X#`

## Conflicting sources

When sources disagree, cite both and state which one you use and why:

> The press release reports [200 patients](url1) while ClinicalTrials.gov
> shows [180 enrolled](url2). We use 180 (official registry).

## Before finalizing

Confirm every quantitative claim, competitor stage, date, and efficacy
figure carries an inline link to a specific (not homepage) URL, and that
conflicting sources are acknowledged. Do not write "studies show" without
naming the studies; do not backfill citations after writing.
